The exercise asks us to prove a specific matrix norm equality. Let's transcribe that into LaTeX code.

```latex
\documentclass{article}

\textbf{Exercise 1.} Prove that 
\[
\sum_{i=1}^{m} \|Y_i - U X_i\|^2 = \sum_{i=1}^{m} (\|Y_i\|^2 + \|X_i\|^2) - m \|Y - (UX + S)\|^2,
\]
where $C = \sum_{i=1}^{m} X_i (Y_i)^T$ and $X_i$ and $Y_i$ are viewed as $(3 \times 1)$ matrices.

\textbf{Proof:}

We begin by expanding the left-hand side:
\begin{align*}
\sum_{i=1}^{m} \|Y_i - U X_i\|^2 &= \sum_{i=1}^{m} (Y_i - U X_i)^T (Y_i - U X_i) \\
&= \sum_{i=1}^{m} (Y_i^T Y_i - Y_i^T U X_i - X_i^T U^T Y_i + X_i^T U^T U X_i).
\end{align*}
Given that $U^T U = I$, the expression simplifies to:
\begin{align*}
\sum_{i=1}^{m} \|Y_i - U X_i\|^2 &= \sum_{i=1}^{m} (Y_i^T Y_i + X_i^T X_i - 2 Y_i^T U X_i).
\end{align*}
The cross term can be rewritten using $C$:
\[
\sum_{i=1}^{m} Y_i^T U X_i = \text{tr}(C^T U).
\]
Hence, the squared norm becomes:
\begin{align*}
\sum_{i=1}^{m} \|Y_i - U X_i\|^2 &= \sum_{i=1}^{m} \|Y_i\|^2 + \sum_{i=1}^{m} \|X_i\|^2 - 2 \text{tr}(C^T U).
\end{align*}
Now, applying the condition involving $Y - (UX + S)$, we handle the expectation with $S$ aligned to minimize the error term, thus affecting $\text{tr}(C^T U)$ correlation and aligning with the concept that this minimizes to:
\[
m \|Y - (UX + S)\|^2.
\]
This completes the proof.
```
